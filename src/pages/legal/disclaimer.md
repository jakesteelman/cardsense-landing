## 3. Card recommendations are approximations

When Cardsense recommends a card for a category or merchant, the recommendation is based on rules we have entered for that card in the catalog. Several limitations apply:

- We compare rewards at **face value** across reward types. A 5x points-earning rate is treated the same as 5% cashback in our ranking. Points typically have different cash value depending on the issuer, the program, and how you redeem them. This means our recommendations may under- or over-value any particular card relative to what is actually best for you. We do our best to assign accurate point valuations to cards in the catalog, but these are necessarily generalizations and may not reflect your personal valuation of those points.
- We do not know your personal valuation of points, your spending habits, your existing rewards balances, your travel preferences, or your tax situation. The Service ignores all of these.
- Bonus categories often have caps, blackout periods, online-only restrictions, merchant-code requirements, enrollment requirements, and other fine print. We try to surface these but cannot guarantee we capture every restriction or that the restrictions we have on file are current. Check your issuer's terms before relying on any recommendation.
- Merchants are matched by category and name. Real-world merchants are often miscategorized by card networks (a gas station's convenience store might code as "gas" or as "grocery" depending on the merchant), and the network's coding — not what Cardsense shows — is what determines how your purchase actually earns rewards.

## 5. Credit tracking is self-reported

The Service tracks card statement credits (dining credits, travel credits, and similar benefits) based on what you tell it. It does not read your card statements or transactions. When the Service shows that you have used $7 of a $10 monthly dining credit, that is because you logged that redemption. It does not mean your card issuer has applied the credit, will apply the credit, or agrees that the credit applies to that purchase.

Credit reset periods, eligibility, and statement timing are determined by your card issuer, not by Cardsense. You should confirm credits posted to your account by reviewing your statement and contacting your issuer if anything is missing.
